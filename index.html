Balancing the Scales: Longterm Health in Life and Work

You want to eat junk food, so you should work out. You're stressed out, so you should find time to unwind. Maybe you have a tense and hard work environment, now humour becomes really important.

Dealing with computers and strict boolean code, maybe we should balance that out with things that make us human. Hand out just a little more compliments, solve something as a group or pair. Simple things like being nice, respectful and open to anyones' ideas.

If you're thinking "duh, yeah those're some pretty obvious things" - me too. I'm thinking the same thing, and we'd probably make a good team.

Blending Ideas for Extraordinary Outcomes

There's this idea of "one and one is three" that I like. Sometimes formulated as "the outcome can be more that the sum of its parts".

Say you're working on something by yourself, you work along and get done with the thing. Imaging someone else doing that task, they might be taking another route and achieveing slightly different results. That's "one and one is two".

Now imaging you're doing the same work, but together. You're brainstorming and discussing ideas, solutions and routes - the outcome may become something neither of you would have thought of individually - that's three. The level up that cooperation can lead to, and part of the power of valuing creativity and our human qualities.
